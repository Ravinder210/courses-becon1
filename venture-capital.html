Course Descriptions

Unlock the secrets of venture capital with this beginner-level workshop designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of the VC ecosystem. Learn deal sourcing, startup evaluation, due diligence, and term sheet negotiation. Participants will explore real-world case studies, role-playing scenarios, and portfolio management strategies, gaining the skills to break into the competitive VC industry.

Requirements for The Course

  • No prior experience in finance or VC is necessary.
  • An interest in startups, entrepreneurship, or investment is highly recommended.
  • Participants should bring a laptop for research and group activities.

Introduction to Venture Capital

Overview of the venture capital industry. Lecture Role and responsibilities of a venture capitalist. Lecture Understanding the startup ecosystem. Lecture Group discussion on recent trends in venture capital. Activity

Deal Sourcing and Evaluation

Strategies for sourcing potential investment opportunities. Lecture Criteria for evaluating startups. Lecture Conducting market research and analysis. Lecture Case study analysis of a successful startup investment. Activity

Due Diligence Process

Steps involved in conducting due diligence. Lecture Assessing financial statements and business models. Lecture Legal and compliance considerations. Lecture Simulation of a due diligence process for a hypothetical startup. Activity

Term Sheets and Deal Structuring

Key components of a term sheet. Lecture Negotiation tactics and strategies. Lecture Understanding valuation and equity stakes. Lecture Role-playing exercise on negotiating a term sheet. Activity

Portfolio Management

Strategies for managing a portfolio of investments. Lecture Monitoring startup performance and providing support. Lecture Planning for exits and returns. Lecture Group project on creating a portfolio management plan. Activity

Legal and Ethical Considerations

Understanding legal frameworks in venture capital. Lecture Ethical dilemmas and best practices. Lecture Intellectual property rights and protections. Lecture Discussion on real-world legal and ethical case studies. Activity

Networking and Building Relationships

Importance of networking in venture capital. Lecture Building relationships with entrepreneurs and co-investors. Lecture Leveraging professional networks for deal flow. Lecture Networking simulation with peer feedback. Activity

Career Pathways in Venture Capital

Different roles within a venture capital firm. Lecture Skills and qualifications required. Lecture Strategies for breaking into the industry. Lecture Panel discussion with experienced venture capitalists. Activity Networking session to connect with peers and share aspirations. Activity
