Course Descriptions

Dive into the essentials of UI/UX Design in this interactive, offline workshop. Perfect for beginners, this course covers user research, prototyping, and visual design principles. Learn the art of creating user personas, wireframes, and responsive layouts. Use tools like Figma to design engaging user interfaces and leave with portfolio-ready projects. This workshop is ideal for aspiring designers looking to enter the industry with strong foundational skills.

Requirements for The Course

  • No prior design experience is needed.
  • Basic familiarity with any design tool (e.g., Figma, Adobe XD) is a plus but not required.
  • Participants should bring a laptop with Figma installed.

Welcome, Icebreaker, and Workshop Overview (30 minutes)

Introduction to Design and its importance in product success. Lecture Overview of the day’s agenda and learning outcomes. Lecture Icebreaker: “Design Detective” – Participants analyze and share thoughts on the usability of everyday objects like water bottles or mobile apps. Activity

Fundamentals of UX Design (1 hour)

Understanding human needs and problem-solving through design. Lecture Basics of user experience (UX) design principles. Lecture Introduction to the Design Thinking process. Lecture Group activity: Teams identify usability issues in a common app or product (e.g., a ticket booking app). Activity

User Research and Personas (1 hour)

Conducting basic user interviews and surveys. Lecture Creating user personas and mapping their journeys. Lecture Hands-on exercise: Teams create a user persona and a journey map for a sample problem (e.g., improving the checkout process on an e-commerce site) Activity

Interactive Quiz and Networking Break (30 minutes)

Quiz on foundational design concepts and tools, with prizes for top scorers. Activity

Ideation and Prototyping (1 hour 30 minutes)

Techniques for brainstorming and generating creative solutions. Lecture Basics of wireframing and prototyping using tools like paper, sketches, or Figma. Lecture Hands-on prototyping: Participants sketch low-fidelity wireframes for their chosen design solution. Activity

Typography, Colors, and Design Systems (1 hour)

Introduction to typography and its impact on user perception. Lecture Using colors and gradients effectively. Lecture Basics of creating consistent design systems. Lecture Team task: Design a basic webpage layout emphasizing typography and color harmony. Activity

Usability Testing and Feedback (1 hour)

Basics of usability testing. Lecture Gathering and incorporating user feedback into design iterations. Lecture Participants test their wireframes within their teams and gather feedback for improvements. Activity

Wrap-Up and Networking (30 minutes)

Summary of key learnings and next steps for aspiring designers. Lecture Resources for continuing the design journey (tools, courses, communities). Lecture Open Q&A with facilitators. Activity Networking session to connect with peers and share aspirations. Activity
